Overview

The Ilm Essentials is an 8-month foundational certificate in Islamic studies. This program aims to equip participants with essential knowledge and skills to become effective role models and contribute to the betterment of society.

The courses program under Youth Club was introduced as an essential tool to fulfil the vision and mission requirements of the organisation. It is intended to create individuals, who are not only aware of the current affairs of the world but have keen insight and possess the power to express themselves through the written word as well as emanate a level of vocal eloquence so that they can defend their Religion at a personal level.

Apply Now!

Learning Outcomes

  • Develop a well-informed sense of what we as Muslims should believe & practice.
  • Develop a strong connection with the life of the Prophet ﷺ.
  • Learn how to maximize your psychological, physical, and spiritual health.
  • Acquire primary-level proficiency in Quranic Arabic pronunciation and meaning.
  • Learn the practical rulings of Islamic Jurisprudence.

Eligibility

To enrol in the Ilm Essentials course, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Completed Matriculation or O-Level.
  • Age range of 16 to 28 years.
  • Proficiency in both English and Urdu.

How to Apply

  • Access the online application form.
  • Review the instructions and submit the application fee.
  • Complete and submit the application form.
  • You will receive a confirmation email and be contacted by a member of the registration team.
  • Your interview date and time will be scheduled for.
  • Our instructors will determine your selection for the program during the interview.
  • Three working days after the interview, you will be notified via email regarding your selection status for the program.

Degree Criteria

To be awarded the Ilm Essentials certificate, students must have:

Minimum CGPA

Maintain a CGPA of at least 2.5 throughout the course.

Evaluation Process

Submit a post-course survey as part of the program's evaluation process.

25 Credit Hours

Pass courses totalling at least 25 credit hours, including all those specified as Core courses.
